How Much Is That Leak Costing You? (Golden State Water Rates)

Most online drip calculators use a generic national water rate. This one uses Golden State Water's actual 2026 tariff, including the CPUC surcharges that add to every CCF, plus the City of Placentia's current sewer rate. The combined marginal cost works out to about 85 cents per 100 gallons, so even a small faucet leak adds up faster than the base rate alone suggests.

A running toilet is the most common source of high bills in Placentia homes. If the calculator confirms a significant cost, the Hidden Water Leak Detector can help you confirm whether the meter is actively spinning with all fixtures off.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a dripping faucet cost per year in Placentia?

A faucet dripping at 60 drops per minute wastes about 5 gallons per day. At Golden State Water Tier 1 rates including CPUC surcharges plus City of Placentia sewer charges, that is about $15 per year. Multiple fixtures multiply the cost proportionally.

What are Golden State Water surcharges?

Five CPUC-authorized surcharges are currently added to every CCF of water used, totaling about 91 cents per CCF on top of the base quantity rate. These include the CAP offset, WRAM/MCBA amortization, and interim rate true-up adjustments.

Does an irrigation leak raise my sewer bill?

No. Outdoor water draining to the yard does not flow to the sanitary sewer system. A sprinkler or irrigation leak costs only the Golden State Water portion.

What are City of Placentia sewer rates?

As of FY 2025-26, the City of Placentia charges 55 cents per CCF on actual monthly water consumption, plus a flat $10 per month capital improvement fee that does not change with usage.
