Frequently Asked Questions

What are the signs of a slab leak in Placentia?

The most common signs are an unexplained bill spike, running-water sounds with all fixtures off, warm floor spots, low pressure throughout the home, and wet or buckling flooring. A spinning meter with all water off confirms active flow.

Why do Placentia homes get pinhole copper leaks?

Most of Placentia was built with copper plumbing now 50 to 70 years old. Golden State Water tests at 12 to 18 grains per gallon of hardness, the highest in our California network. That mineral load causes internal pitting in copper pipe walls over decades.

How do plumbers find a slab leak without breaking concrete?

Acoustic listening, electronic pressure testing, and thermal imaging locate the failure precisely before any concrete is opened.

Why do Placentia insurance claims for leaks get denied?

Most denials cite lack of documentation or a finding that the leak was gradual. Photograph visible symptoms, save meter readings, and keep Golden State Water bills before calling a plumber.
