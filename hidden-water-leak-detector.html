Find a Hidden Water Leak in Your Placentia Home (Water Meter Test)

The water meter is the most reliable way to confirm a hidden leak. Golden State Water has served Placentia since 1929, and the bill includes both a tiered quantity rate and several CPUC-authorized surcharges, so the true cost of a leak is often higher than the base rate alone suggests.

A slab leak or supply-line failure running continuously can waste hundreds of gallons per day without any visible drip. The meter test below quantifies exactly how much water you are losing and what it is costing on real Golden State Water and City of Placentia rates.

How to Run the Meter Test

  1. Turn off every water fixture and appliance in the home, including the irrigation system and ice makers.
  2. Locate your Golden State Water meter at the curb and write down the current reading in CCF or gallons.
  3. Wait at least 2 hours without using any water. Longer is better for detecting slow leaks.
  4. Read the meter again and enter both numbers below.

What the Results Mean

If the test shows an active leak, the severity tier and likely cause help narrow down the source. A running toilet is the most common cause of slow losses. Larger losses, particularly anything over 8 gallons per hour with all fixtures off, point to a supply pipe or underground line failure. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I read my Golden State Water meter?

Locate the meter box at the curb, lift the lid, and read the dial showing CCF or gallons. The small triangular flow indicator spins whenever water is moving through the meter.

Why does my Golden State Water bill have extra surcharges?

Golden State Water is regulated by the CPUC, which authorizes the company to recover specific costs through separate surcharges. As of January 2026, five active surcharges total about 91 cents per CCF on top of the base quantity rate.

Does a leak raise my Placentia sewer bill?

Yes. The City of Placentia bills sewer charges on actual monthly water consumption at 55 cents per CCF. Any water running through your meter also appears on your sewer bill.

Can a slab leak cause a high water bill in Placentia?

Yes. Slab leaks run continuously and can waste thousands of gallons per month before showing symptoms. A meter test with all fixtures off is the fastest way to confirm an active leak.
